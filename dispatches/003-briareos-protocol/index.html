The Briareos Protocol

DISPATCH 003 // 2026.04.02 // FIELD CORRESPONDENT: R. TANAKA

Tanaka's recovery team found the unit three clicks east of the old Olympus perimeter wall, half-buried in rubble from a structure that had collapsed sometime in the last decade. A Hecatonchires-class combat chassis — military designation BRIAREOS — still operational, still armed, its optics tracking the team's approach with the patient precision of something that had been watching for a very long time. The unit's service records showed a decommission date eleven years prior. Nobody had told it to stop.

The operative crouching beside it wore gear that didn't match any active unit's loadout. Salvaged armor, modified optics bolted onto a headset that looked hand-built, an Olympus tactical badge that hadn't been issued in years. She identified herself as former S.W.A.T. but refused to give a unit number or current affiliation. She said they'd been running independent operations in the dead zones — clearing out infrastructure that had been "repurposed" by something she wouldn't name. When Tanaka asked what she meant by repurposed, she pointed east into the dark and said nothing.

The imaging data recovered from the unit's onboard storage is what elevated this dispatch to classified. Forty-seven hours of continuous recording, most of it corrupted, but the fragments that survived show something standing in the dark of a collapsed service tunnel. Tall, wrong proportions, limbs that branch and re-branch into filaments that glow faintly with their own light. The unit's threat assessment system had tagged it with a designation Tanaka had never seen before — ULTIMATE ORGANISM, PRACTICALLY INDESTRUCTIBLE — and had been tracking it for eleven years. Watching it grow. Documenting its changes. Following a protocol that was never officially issued, for a threat that doesn't officially exist.

End of Dispatch
